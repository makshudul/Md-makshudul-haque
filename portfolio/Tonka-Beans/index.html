About

Tonka Beans are produced by The Cumaru Tree, sometimes referred to as the Kumaru Tree. It is a flowering tree that grows in a relatively small geographic region, specifically in Central America and a small section of Northern South America. Carbon dating of fallen trees confirms that the species is over 1000 years old. Tonka Beans are the large black seeds of the Cumaru Tree.

Tonka Beans are harvested from the “fruit” of the Cumaru Tree. The trees flower in early spring, March through May. The Tonka Bean “fruit” which are seed pods ripen from June to July. After harvesting the seeds are removed from the pod and laid out to dry naturally. After a drying period that normally lasts for 2-4 days, the seeds are hand selected with the largest packaged as Tonka Beans for you.
